So, some basic calculations:

if you were college age in 1990, you were probably 40-45 in 2013, when PIACC did its first adult literacy tests. Then, in the data, the PIACC scores for 40-45 year olds is around 220. Which tells us that 0.15*X + 0.85*Y = 220, where X is the score for college goers and Y is the score for non college-goers.

Now, if you fast forward to 2011, the people who were college age in 2011 are probably around 30-35 in 2023, and their PIACC scores are around 238. So that tell us that 0.5X1 + 0.5Y1 = 238, which at first looks like there has been some improvement, but you have to also account for the fact that scores fall with age, and if you calibrate that properly, you get back to around 220! So, now you have 0.5X1 + 0.5Y1 = 220 as well, which is bizarre and suggests massive declines in the actual gains from going to college.
